Less Money, More Recycling

It's just a working title, but it does look like wealthier neighborhoods just aren't recycling like they used to.

By Sawyer Click, Aseem Shukla and Frida Cai

Published Dec. 2018 | Repo

You might expect the Financial district at the Wall Street are filled with highly-educated people who have mastered the practice of recycling. The truth is that the rich are caring less and less about trash disposal.

A volunteer at the nonprofit county recycling organization in Montana, Britting believed that the globalization of recycling catalyzed unprecedented challenges and doubts to local systems. "Back when it first started, people were optimistic about recycling," he remarked on ABC News, "Everyone thinks they’re being recycled, but recycling is a market-driven industry.”

Wealthier Neighborhoods Have Curbed Their Recycling

More affluent neighborhood's are recycling less than they did a decade ago. But the opposite is true for less affluent neighborhoods.
